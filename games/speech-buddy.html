🔬 Our Methodology

Speech Buddy is built on decades of peer-reviewed speech-language pathology research. Here's the science behind every feature.

🎯 Core Vocabulary Approach
We prioritize core words (want, more, go, stop, help, all done) over content words (cat, banana). Research shows just 50 core words make up ~80% of what we say daily. These functional words give your child the power to communicate needs immediately.
Banajee, M., DiCarlo, C., & Stricklin, S. (2003). "Core Vocabulary Determination for Toddlers." AAC Journal.
🧠 Naturalistic Developmental Behavioral Interventions (NDBI)
Words are taught within daily routines (mealtime, bath, play) — not in isolation. Research shows children learn language best in meaningful, motivating contexts where they're naturally engaged.
Schreibman, L. et al. (2015). "Naturalistic Developmental Behavioral Interventions." Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders.
👂 Hanen "It Takes Two to Talk" Principles
Our parent coaching follows the OWL strategy: Observe what your child is interested in, Wait for them to communicate, Listen to their attempts. We model words — never demand "Say ___!"
Pepper, J. & Weitzman, E. (2004). "It Takes Two to Talk." The Hanen Centre.
🔤 Phonological Development Hierarchy
Words are organized by sound difficulty. Early developing sounds (/p/, /b/, /m/, /d/, /n/, /t/, /w/, /h/) come first. We don't expect a 2-year-old to say "rabbit" perfectly — "wabbit" is developmentally appropriate.
Sander, E. (1972). "When Are Speech Sounds Learned?" Journal of Speech and Hearing Disorders.
🌍 Multilingual Advantage
We support English, Russian, and Kyrgyz because research confirms that bilingualism does NOT cause speech delays. Count vocabulary across ALL languages. Continue using every home language — it strengthens cognitive development.
Petitto, L.A. et al. (2001). "Bilingual signed and spoken language acquisition." PNAS. ASHA Position Statement on Bilingualism.

Our 7 Principles

  • 1 Model, don't demand. Say the word naturally — never force "Say cat!" This creates pressure and resistance. Simply model: "Look, a cat! Cat."
  • 2 Accept approximations. "Ba" for ball, "muh" for more — these ARE words. Celebrate every attempt, then model the full word back.
  • 3 Repetition is everything. Children need to hear a word 50-100+ times before producing it. This app tracks exposure to help you stay consistent.
  • 4 Follow their lead. If your child reaches for the dog, talk about the dog — not the cat you planned to practice.
  • 5 Keep it short. 5-10 minutes is ideal. End while they're still engaged, not after they've lost interest.
  • 6 Use all senses. Pair words with gestures, sounds, touch. "Splash!" during bath, "Mmm, yummy!" during eating.
  • 7 Routines are your superpower. Predictable daily activities (meals, bath, bedtime) are the #1 context for language learning.

🚩 When to See a Professional

  • No babbling by 12 months
  • No gestures (pointing, waving) by 12 months
  • No single words by 16 months
  • No two-word phrases by 24 months
  • Loss of previously acquired words at any age
  • Not responding to their name consistently by 12 months

Source: ASHA (American Speech-Language-Hearing Association). Early intervention is most effective before age 3. Trust your instincts — you know your child best.
